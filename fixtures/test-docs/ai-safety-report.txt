AI Safety Research Report - Q4 2025

Executive Summary

This report examines the current state of AI safety research, focusing on alignment techniques,
interpretability advances, and risk mitigation strategies. The findings suggest that while
significant progress has been made in mechanistic interpretability, challenges remain in
scaling these approaches to frontier models.

Key Findings

1. Constitutional AI methods have shown promise in reducing harmful outputs by 73% compared
   to baseline RLHF approaches, while maintaining helpfulness scores within 5% of unaligned models.

2. Sparse autoencoders have enabled researchers to identify and manipulate individual features
   in transformer models with up to 2 billion parameters, though scaling beyond this remains
   an active area of research.

3. Red-teaming exercises conducted across 15 organizations revealed that multi-turn adversarial
   attacks remain the most effective method for eliciting unsafe behavior, highlighting the need
   for improved context-window safety mechanisms.

Recommendations

- Invest in scalable interpretability tools that can keep pace with model size increases
- Develop standardized benchmarks for measuring alignment robustness
- Establish cross-organizational sharing of red-team findings through secure channels
